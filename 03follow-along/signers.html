Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Several notable figures signed the Declaration of Independence in 1776, and among the most famous are:

John Hancock
As the President of the Continental Congress, Hancock's signature is the largest and most prominent on the Declaration. His bold signing made him one of the most recognizable figures in American history.
Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence, Jefferson's eloquent words about liberty and equality became foundational to American political philosophy.
Benjamin Franklin
A key diplomat, inventor, and statesman, Franklin was instrumental in securing foreign aid during the Revolutionary War and played a vital role in the Continental Congress.
John Adams
A passionate advocate for independence and a leader in the Continental Congress, Adams later became the second president of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, Samuel was a leader of the American Revolution and one of the key figures in organizing resistance against British rule.
Richard Henry Lee
Lee proposed the resolution for independence to the Continental Congress, making him a central figure in the push for the Declaration.
Francis Lewis
A signer from New York, Lewis was a successful merchant and a strong supporter of the colonies' independence.
Charles Carroll
From Maryland, Carroll was the wealthiest signer and one of the most committed to the cause of independence.

These figures were among the 56 signers, and they represented a diverse group of individuals, from lawyers to farmers, who were united in their desire for independence from Britain.
